% ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Overview}
% ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This chapter presents the full experimental findings for the Serverless Spark IoT
Framework, covering training convergence, adaptive policy behaviour, comparative
benchmarking, 24-hour workload simulation, and the impact of the OpenFaaS serverless
deployment. All experiments were conducted in the containerised testbed described in
Chapter~\ref{chap:architecture}, with the Two-Phase RL system (PPO + TD3) deployed as
the \texttt{rl-scaling-function} via OpenFaaS.

The chapter is structured as follows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Section~\ref{sec:exp_setup} --- Experimental setup and methodology.
  \item Section~\ref{sec:training} --- Training dynamics and convergence (PPO + TD3).
  \item Section~\ref{sec:policy_demo} --- Policy decision demo across three workload regimes.
  \item Section~\ref{sec:stats} --- Statistical evaluation across 20 episodes.
  \item Section~\ref{sec:baseline} --- Comparative evaluation against four baselines.
  \item Section~\ref{sec:24h} --- 24-hour real IoT workload simulation.
  \item Section~\ref{sec:openfaas_results} --- OpenFaaS cost impact (scale-to-zero).
  \item Sections~\ref{sec:discussion}--\ref{sec:summary_results} --- Discussion, limitations, and summary.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
% ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Why RL Outperforms Threshold-Based Baselines}

The fundamental weakness of all static or reactive policies is their inability to
\textit{anticipate} workload changes. Dexter (HPA) responds only after CPU crosses a
threshold, incurring the full 45--60 second cold-start penalty before new executors
are ready. During the 9 AM mega-spike, this means approximately 1,950 messages are
dropped before the cluster recovers.

The RL agent avoids this by acting on the burst-probability signal $P_{burst}$,
computed from the 7-feature Pattern Extractor, up to 45 seconds \textit{before} the
burst arrives. The \texttt{PRE\_WARM} action triggers executor container creation in
advance, so by the time the data tsunami hits, the cluster is already fully provisioned.

\subsection{The Role of the Two-Phase Hierarchy}

A single-phase PPO with fixed weights ($\alpha{=}0.4, \beta{=}0.4, \gamma{=}0.2$)
would struggle with the night-idle scenario: balanced weights create incentive to keep
some executors running to maintain throughput, even when they serve virtually no traffic.
The TD3 Meta-Controller removes this conflict by raising $\alpha$ to 0.80 during idle
periods, forcing cost minimisation, and switching to $\beta{=}0.80$ during bursts to
prioritise latency at any cost. This context-sensitivity is the core reason the system
achieves both 95.6\% idle-cost reduction \textit{and} 0\% SLA violations simultaneously.

\subsection{OpenFaaS as an Architectural Enabler}

Deploying the RL engine as an OpenFaaS function is not merely a deployment choice ---
it is what makes scale-to-zero feasible at the \textit{control plane} level. When
there are no active IoT streams, both the Spark executors \textit{and} the RL
optimizer are terminated, achieving a true zero-cost idle state. This is impossible
with embedded RL (Spark Driver always runs) or with Kubernetes-native deployments
(minimum replica $\geq$1 incurs continuous billing).

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:summary_results}
% ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This chapter presented a comprehensive empirical evaluation of the Serverless Spark
IoT Framework with Two-Phase RL and OpenFaaS integration. The key findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Training convergence:} All three PPO runs converged reliably within
        50,000 steps, improving rollout reward by $+$540 points. The TD3 Meta-Controller
        successfully learned to shift from cost-priority to latency-priority as training
        progressed.

  \item \textbf{SLA compliance:} RL-Spark achieved \textbf{100\% SLA compliance}
        across all 68 evaluation episodes --- the only policy among five to do so.

  \item \textbf{Cost efficiency:} Total 24-hour costs were reduced by \textbf{36.5\%}
        (\$15.05 saving), with idle periods costing \$0.50 vs.\ \$11.25 for the
        traditional fixed approach.

  \item \textbf{Latency:} P95 latency improved from 1,534 ms to 306 ms
        ($-$80.1\%), far exceeding the 1,000 ms SLA threshold.

  \item \textbf{Proactive scaling:} The \texttt{PRE\_WARM} action, guided by the
        7-feature Pattern Extractor, eliminated all 7 cold-start events observed in
        the baseline.

  \item \textbf{Throughput:} Peak throughput increased 2.2$\times$ (750 msg/s to
        1,650 msg/s) thanks to dynamic scale-out up to 25 executors during bursts.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent These results establish that hierarchical reinforcement learning, deployed
through a serverless function gateway, is a practical and highly effective approach
to autonomous resource management in real-time IoT data processing at scale.
